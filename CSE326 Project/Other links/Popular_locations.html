New Delhi

New Delhi is an urban district located in the city of Delhi. New Delhi serves as the capital of India and the seat of all three branches of the Government of India.

Jaipur

Jaipur is the capital and the largest city of the Indian state of Rajasthan. Jaipur is also known as the Pink City, due to the dominant color scheme of its buildings.

Mumbai

Mumbai, formerly Bombay, is big. It’s full of dreamers and hard-labourers, starlets and stray dogs and exotic birds, artists and servants, fisherfolk and crorepatis (millionaires), and lots and lots of people..

Bangluru

Bangalore, officially known as Bengaluru, is the capital of the Indian state of Karnataka. It has a population of over ten million, making it a megacity and the third-most populous city and fifth-most populous urban agglomeration in India.

Kolkata

Kolkata is known for its literary, artistic, and revolutionary heritage; as the former capital of India, it was the birth place of mordern indian literary nad artistic thought. Kolkata has been called the "City of Furious, Creative Energy" as well as the "Cultural capital of India".

Chennai

Chennai, also known as Madras, is the capital of the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. Located on the Coromandel Coast off the Bay of Bengal, it is the biggest cultural, economic and educational centre of south India. According to the 2011 Indian census, it is the sixth-most populous city and fourth-most populous urban agglomeration in India.

Hyderabad

Hyderabad is the capital and largest city of the Indian state of Telangana and de jure capital of Andhra Pradesh. Occupying 650 square kilometres along the banks of the Musi River, located on the Deccan Plateau in the northern part of South India.
